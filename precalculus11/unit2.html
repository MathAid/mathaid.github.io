Unit 2 - Rational Functions & Expressions

2.1 - Introduction to Rational Expressions & Restrictions

Rational expressions are typically the quotient of two polynomials. Examples of rational expressions are: 2+x/3+x or x-2/x2+9



Restrictions in rational expressions are numbers that the variable cannot be equal to or the expression may result in being an undefined value. To find restrictions, we would take the denominator of the expression and convert it into an express which does not equal to zero.
For example, the equation x+3/x-3:
x-3≠0
x≠3
So from this process we can are able to see that in this expression, x cannot be equal to 3 or else it would be an undefined value.
