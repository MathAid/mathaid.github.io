5.1 - Solving Quadratic with Factoring

A quadratic function's "solutions" are the x-intercepts of the graph. We can find the solutions by graphing the parabola but there are actually much more conventional methods. In unit 5.1, we will take a look at the factoring method.



The factoring method will be using the zero factor property.


Video from Angiewvc learning about zero.

Converting general form to standard form (vertex form) example: f(x) = x2 + 4x + 3

Find the perfect square (create the perfect trinomial

In this case, we can ignore 3 and just look at x2 + 4x. What number can make x2 + 4x a perfect trinomial? We can turn it into f(x) = (x2 + 4x + 4 - 4) + 3 which would be f(x) = (x2 + 4x + 4) - 4 + 3.

Fact the perfect trinomial

Since we created the perfect trinomial, we have to now factor it and turn it into vertex form. We would have to turn f(x) = (x2 + 4x + 4) - 4 + 3 into f(x) = (x + 2)2 - 1
