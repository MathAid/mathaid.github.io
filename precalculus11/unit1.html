1.1 - Factoring x2 + bx + c

When factoring any x2 + bx + c equation, you must keep these 3 rules in mind


1. The standard equation for the factoring basic trionomials x2 + bx + c would mainly be the (x + a)(x + b) as it is the product of x2 + bx + c

2. The first term which is x2 in this case, that would be the first term of (x + a)(x + b) as it is the product of x and x

3. The coefficient of the middle term should be the sum of a and b and the last term of trinomial should be the product of a and b

Factoring ax2 + bx + c


Similar to factoring x2 + bx + c equation, factoring ax2 + bx + c will be slightly different. There are many methods in factoring these kind of equations but from my own experience, the easiest and fastest way of factoring ax2 + bx + c is the AC method.
Lets take a look at 2x2 + 5x + 2

Here is a video I made on the ac method since I believe that a video explanation is far better than just plain text.


1.You would want to multiple the a term with the c term which is 2 x 2. The equation would then be, x2 + 5x + 4

2.Factor the trinomial (x2 + 5x + 4) turning it into (x + 4)(x + 1)

3. Then you would divide the values by a (2) and simplifying turning the equation into (x + 4/2)(x + 1/2)

4. Looking at (x + 4/2), you are able to simplify it to (x + 2). However for (x + 1/2) you aren't able to simplify meaning you would have to move the denominator to the front (2x + 1).

5. Putting the equations together, it will be (x + 2)(2x + 1)

Adding & Subtracting Radicals

Here is a quick video by MathHelp.com.

The main rule about adding and subtracting radicals are that you must have similar terms and so you can add and/or subtract the coefficient. Similar terms are (√7 and 2√7, 2√x and 2√x).
However unlike terms would be (3√x and 2√x)


If the coefficient is not the same, we may use what we have learned in unit 1.2 to simplify the radicals until it becomes the same coefficients. Example would be 2√7 + 3√28


To add the two terms together, we must simplify 3√28 to 3√2x2x7


Which then would turn to 2√7 + 6√7
