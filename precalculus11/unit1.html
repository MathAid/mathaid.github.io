Unit 1 - Factoring and Radicals

1.1 - Factoring x2 + bx + c

When factoring any x2 + bx + c equation, you must keep these 3 rules in mind


  1. The standard equation for the factoring basic trionomials x2 + bx + c would mainly be the (x + a)(x + b) as it is the product of x2 + bx + c

  2. The first term which is x2 in this case, that would be the first term of (x + a)(x + b) as it is the product of x and x

  3. The coefficient of the middle term should be the sum of a and b and the last term of trinomial should be the product of a and b
